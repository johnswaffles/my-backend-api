# Cozy Builder Visual Direction

## North Star
- Soft handcrafted diorama
- Cozy village-builder, not toy-plastic city sim
- Warm golden-hour atmosphere
- Rotation-safe visuals with no baked directional lighting
- Buildings, roads, and nature should feel like they belong to the same miniature world

## Shape language
- Rounded roofs, softened corners, chunky silhouettes
- Parcels are as important as buildings
- Every lot should feel lovingly dressed with paths, fences, shrubs, flowers, and yard details
- Roads should feel stitched into the land, not stamped on top of it

## Material language
- Muted earthy base colors
- Cream plaster, faded timber, mossy greens, dusty stone, weathered brick, sun-softened paint
- Avoid glossy plastic surfaces
- Prefer rough, velvety, chalky, and matte finishes

## Lighting language
- Real-time lighting only
- Golden directional sun with soft fill light
- Gentle fog and warm atmosphere
- Soft contact shadows under parcels, trees, props, people, and cars
- Rotation must never break the illusion

## World-detail rules
- Blank land should still feel alive
- Shorelines need stones, reeds, and soft edge variation
- Open lots should carry little hints of life: tufts, flowers, path scars, meadow shapes
- Town growth should automatically beautify itself wherever possible

## UI mood
- Slim, translucent, premium
- Calm and supportive, not noisy
- More “beautiful tool tray” than “prototype debug HUD”

## Inspiration target
- A cozy miniature settlement with tactile, hand-placed charm
- Soft procedural beautification that makes the player feel clever and creative
- “Everything looks good by default” should be the rule
